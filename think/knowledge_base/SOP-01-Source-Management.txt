DOCUMENT ID: SOP-01
TITLE: Standard Operating Procedure for Power Source Management
VERSION: 1.2
DATE: 2023-10-27
---------------------------------------------------------------------

1.  **Primary Goal:** To minimize the carbon footprint of the society's energy consumption while ensuring cost-effectiveness and grid stability.

2.  **Source Priority Hierarchy:** The default operational priority for power sources is as follows:
    2.1. **Priority 1: On-site Solar Generation.** If solar generation (`solar_kw`) is greater than the current total load (`load_kw`), the system must operate exclusively on solar power.
    2.2. **Priority 2: Stored Battery Power.** Utilize battery power when conditions in Section 3 are met.
    2.3. **Priority 3: National Grid.** Use grid power only when solar and battery are insufficient or when grid conditions are exceptionally favorable (see Section 4).

3.  **Battery Discharge Protocol:**
    3.1. **Carbon-based Discharge:** Initiate battery discharge if `solar_kw` is less than `load_kw` AND the grid carbon intensity (`grid_carbon_intensity`) is above 400 gCO2eq/kWh. The goal is to avoid drawing from a "dirty" grid.
    3.2. **Peak Shaving / Cost Avoidance:** Initiate battery discharge during designated peak tariff hours (17:00 - 20:00) if the battery state of charge (`battery_soc`) is above 60% (0.60), regardless of carbon intensity, to reduce costs.
    3.3. **Discharge Cut-off:** Cease battery discharge and switch to the grid if `battery_soc` falls below 20% (0.20), unless in an emergency state (see EMG-01). This 20% buffer is reserved for critical situations.

4.  **Grid Import Protocol:**
    4.1. **Standard Use:** It is permissible to draw power from the grid when `solar_kw` is less than `load_kw` and battery discharge conditions (Section 3) are not met.
    4.2. **Opportunistic Charging (Green Grid):** If `grid_carbon_intensity` is below 150 gCO2eq/kWh AND `battery_soc` is below 80% (0.80), the system is authorized to draw power from the grid to charge the battery, even if solar power is sufficient to cover the load. This is known as "green charging".

5.  **Grid Export Prohibition:** Under no circumstances is this microgrid authorized to export power back to the national grid. If `solar_kw` significantly exceeds `load_kw` and the battery is fully charged (`battery_soc` > 98%), solar generation must be curtailed to match the load.
